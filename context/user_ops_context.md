## Channel Configuration

### Delivery Platforms
- **Active platforms**: Meituan (65% of delivery revenue), Ele.me (30%), Didi Food (5%)
- **Commission structure**: Meituan 21%, Ele.me 18%, Didi Food 15%
- **Ranking factors**: Fulfillment rate, customer rating, response time, coupon depth
- **Known pressure point**: Meituan algorithm deprioritizes stores with < 4.3 rating or < 90% fulfillment rate

### Group Buying
- **Active deals**: 50% off first-order (Meituan), 30% off limited menu (Ele.me)
- **Discount tolerance**: Will not go below 35% total discount (brand + platform combined) — margin floor
- **Redemption rate target**: 68% actual redemption on 50% deals (industry avg: 55%)
- **Problem history**: 70% off deals in Q1 2024 attracted coupon chasers — LTV of these users was ¥12 avg vs ¥89 for organic

### Membership Program
- **Active members**: ~210,000 registered (across WeChat mini-program + app)
- **Avg lifetime value**: ¥127 (based on 18-month cohort analysis)
- **Dormancy threshold**: 60 days inactive → marked "Dormant"
- **Current dormancy rate**: 41% of members are dormant (≈86,000 dormant accounts)
- **Points currency**: 1 point = ¥0.01. Members redeem for free add-ons, discounts, or merch
- **Reactivation history**: Previous 90-day reactivation campaigns achieved 8% reactivation rate at ¥3.2 CPA

### In-Store
- **Avg order prep time**: 4.5 minutes
- **Rush hour capacity**: 180 orders/hour/store (theoretical max), 140 effective during peak
- **Staff constraints**: Night shifts (20:00–22:00) understaffed at 12 of 47 stores — kitchen speed drops 30%

## Constraints & Rules

### Margin Protection
- **Minimum gross margin**: 58%
- **Maximum discount depth**: 35% combined (brand + platform)
- **Cost per acquisition ceiling**: ¥5 for new customer acquisition, ¥3.5 for member acquisition, ¥4 for dormancy reactivation

### Brand Safety
- **Cannot do**: Political content, sexual/inappropriate imagery, competitor comparisons, lottery/gambling mechanics
- **Tone requirements**: Fun but not childish, local but not exclusionary, affordable but not cheap
- **Visual guidelines**: Bold red/yellow brand colors, wok imagery, no text-heavy posters

### Operational Limits
- **Max orders per hour per store**: 140 effective during peak
- **Menu items that cannot be promoted**: Seasonal items not yet confirmed, items with < 3-week shelf life
- **Blackout dates**: None — but major campaign launches avoid the last 3 days of any platform settlement period

### Compliance
- **Platform rules**: Must honor all advertised prices; no false urgency; privacy-compliant data use
- **Advertising regulations**: Must disclose any "AI-recommended" features per CAC guidelines
- **Data privacy**: WeChat/Mini Program data cannot be exported to third-party DSPs

## Known Issues & Past Failures

### "70% Flash Sale" (March 2024)
- **What happened**: Ran 70% off limited menu deal on Ele.me. 3,200 orders in 4 hours — store overload, 43% cancellation rate, 1,100 customer complaints
- **Why it failed**: Did not set per-store capacity caps; deal duration too long; no fulfillment rate monitoring
- **Lesson**: Any >50% discount deal MUST have per-store daily caps and real-time kill switch

### "Dormancy Blast — Generic" (November 2024)
- **What happened**: Sent uniform "Come Back!" message to all 86,000 dormant members. 12% open rate, 3.1% conversion
- **Why it failed**: Same message to everyone. Members dormant for 200+ days need different treatment than 60–90 day dormants
- **Lesson**: Segment dormancy depth before messaging. Different cohorts need different offers and creative

### "Influencer Launch — Wrong Audience" (June 2024)
- **What happened**: Partnered with gaming streamer (2M followers). Campaign drove 8,000 new members, but avg order value was ¥22 and 80% never ordered again
- **Why it failed**: Gaming audience self-selected for cheap food; not actual target customer profile
- **Lesson**: Match influencer audience to brand's real customer LTV profile, not raw follower count
