# ST-008: Meituan Commission Negotiation — Rate Increase from 18% to 22%

> **Priority**: HIGH — Decision needed within 2 weeks, account manager call scheduled May 20
> **Submitted by**: Regional Operations + Finance
> **Date**: 2026-05-12

---

## Situation

Meituan's account manager has informed us that our commission rate will increase from **18% to 22%** effective July 1, 2026. This affects our single largest revenue channel: **65% of our total delivery volume (¥1.82M GMV/year) runs through Meituan.**

Current economics:
- Meituan GMV: ¥1.82M/year → commission at 18% = ¥327,600/year
- Proposed commission at 22% = ¥400,400/year
- **Additional cost: ¥72,800/year** (or ¥6,067/month)

Our blended gross margin on delivery is 61%. With the commission increase, effective margin on Meituan orders drops from 43% to 39% — still above our 35% floor, but the trend is concerning.

Meituan has offered a counter-proposal: we can **keep 18% if we commit to running at least 2 Meituan-exclusive promotional campaigns per quarter** (worth ¥50,000+ GMV each in their view). They frame this as "collaborative growth investment."

We have 2 weeks to prepare our negotiation position.

---

## Goals (Ranked)

1. Limit commission increase to ≤ 20% (saving ≥ ¥36,400/year vs. full 22%)
2. Avoid mandatory campaign commitments that erode margin (campaign costs are incremental)
3. Maintain the Meituan partnership — walking away means losing 65% of delivery revenue
4. Negotiate a 12-month rate lock as an alternative or supplement
5. Develop a contingency plan if negotiation fails

---

## Constraints

- ** BATNA (Best Alternative)**: Ele.me currently accounts for 28% of delivery volume. Ele.me's effective commission is 20%. We could theoretically shift more volume to Ele.me, but their Shanghai user base is 15% smaller.
- **Switching cost**: A Meituan store suspension for non-payment of new commission rate would cost ~¥4,500/day in lost GMV.
- **Margin floor**: Cannot let effective delivery margin drop below 35% (board mandate).
- **Time**: Account manager call May 20. Need a prepared position and walk-away point by May 18.
- **Platform leverage**: Meituan knows we depend on them. They know we know this.

---

## Team Positions

**Finance Controller (Wang):**
> "The 22% rate is painful but manageable — we're still above the 35% floor. The real issue is the Ele.me comparison: at 20% commission, we're leaving ¥36,400/year on the table by not having shifted more volume to Ele.me. Our negotiating leverage is: we're willing to increase Meituan volume in exchange for keeping 18%. The 'collaborative growth' campaign requirement could be reframed as us getting discounted commission in exchange for marketing investment. If each campaign generates ¥80K in incremental GMV and costs us ¥4,000 in margin, that's a 20x return on the campaign investment. Maybe we should WANT the campaigns."

**Regional Delivery Lead (Jiang):**
> "I'm nervous about the campaign commitment. 'At least 2 campaigns per quarter' sounds manageable until you realize that campaigns mean discounts, and discounts mean margin erosion beyond what the commission rate already costs. If a campaign averages 10% discount on ¥60K GMV, that's ¥6,000 in discount cost plus the extra 4% commission = ¥8,400 in total incremental cost per campaign. × 8 campaigns/year = ¥67,200. That's more than just accepting the 22% rate. We need to cap the campaign discount at 5% and get Meituan to co-fund 50%."

**Strategy Manager:**
> "The real leverage we have: our Shanghai store consistently ranks in the top 15% of the Meituan QSR category for customer rating and repeat-order rate. We're valuable to Meituan not just for GMV but for their category quality metrics. We should lead with that — 'your quality metrics benefit from our store ranking, that's worth more to you than the extra 4% commission.' Use the Ele.me threat as background, not foreground. Threaten to reduce Meituan allocation to 50% if the rate goes to 22%, giving Ele.me the rest. That costs us ¥9,100/year in extra Ele.me commission at 20%, but it's a credible signal."

---

## Options Under Review

**Option A — "Accept 20%, No Campaign Commitment"**
- Counter-propose: accept 20% commission, reject the mandatory campaign requirement.
- Offer to run 1 optional campaign per quarter (brand-funded, Meituan co-placement only).
- Estimated cost: ¥36,400/year additional commission. No campaign cost.
- Walk-away: 21% with 1 optional campaign.

**Option B — "18% for Volume Commitment"**
- Accept 18% commission with the 2-campaign-per-quarter commitment.
- Negotiate campaign discount cap at 5%, Meituan co-funds 50% of discount cost.
- Estimated: ¥0 additional commission + ~¥12,000/year in capped campaign costs.
- Risk: Campaign costs could creep upward if not tightly contracted.

**Option C — "Walk Away from Meituan Primary"**
- Signal willingness to shift 30% of Meituan volume to Ele.me if 22% rate is non-negotiable.
- Use Ele.me as negotiating counterweight: "We'll allocate 50% to Meituan, 40% to Ele.me, 10% to self-delivery if you insist on 22%."
- Estimated: ¥9,100/year in Ele.me incremental commission, but forces Meituan to reconsider.
