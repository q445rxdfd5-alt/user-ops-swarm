# ST-011: Ele.me Group Buying Deal — Q3 Campaign Design

> **Priority**: MEDIUM — Campaign launch target: July 1, negotiation deadline: May 28
> **Submitted by**: Ele.me Platform Relations + Growth
> **Date**: 2026-05-10

---

## Situation

Ele.me's commercial team has offered Wok & Roll preferential homepage placement in the Shanghai "Lunch Deal Zone" (每天午餐精选) in exchange for running a **group buying deal** at their negotiated terms:

- **Deal structure**: 2-person meal set at ¥58 (regular price: ¥76, 24% off)
- **Platform requirement**: Minimum 200 units/day on deal
- **Placement**: Homepage "Group Buying" banner for 14 consecutive days (July 1-14)
- **Platform co-funding**: Ele.me will contribute 8% of the discount (we fund 16%, platform funds 8%)
- **Your cost per deal**: ¥76 × 16% = ¥12.16 per unit in brand-funded discount
- **Volume guarantee**: Ele.me requires minimum 200 units/day. Below 200 for 3+ consecutive days = placement terminated without refund

Break-even analysis:
- 200 units/day × 14 days = 2,800 total deal orders
- ¥12.16 discount cost × 2,800 = ¥34,048 total brand cost
- ¥58 deal price × 61% margin = ¥35.38 gross margin per unit
- ¥35.38 × 2,800 = ¥99,064 gross margin — ¥34,048 cost = **¥65,016 net contribution**

Store manager concern: Shanghai store can handle 180 standard orders/day. Adding 200 deal orders/day would push us to **380 orders/day** — kitchen cannot sustain that quality. We would need to source fulfillment from 2 additional stores (Pudong + Jing'an) which adds ¥2/delivery in logistics cost.

---

## Goals (Ranked)

1. Generate ≥ ¥120,000 in incremental GMV from the deal period
2. Acquire ≥ 800 new Ele.me users (deal users who have never ordered from Wok & Roll before)
3. Maintain quality rating ≥ 4.5 during deal period (fulfillment risk is highest concern)
4. Ensure deal users convert to full-price repeat orders (target: ≥ 25% return rate at full price within 30 days)
5. Develop Ele.me as a stronger secondary channel (currently 28% of delivery volume)

---

## Constraints

- **Fulfillment cap**: Shanghai store max 180 orders/day without quality compromise
- **Logistics cost**: Fulfilling from additional stores adds ¥2/delivery = ¥5,600 additional cost over 14 days
- **Minimum volume commitment**: 200 units/day for 14 days = 2,800 total. If we average below 200, Ele.me terminates placement.
- **Margin floor**: Deal economics must maintain ≥ 55% gross margin after all costs
- **No brand dilution**: Deal set price cannot be below ¥52 (brand floor for Ele.me positioning)
- **Budget**: Total campaign budget (discounts + logistics + content) ≤ ¥50,000

---

## Team Positions

**Growth Manager (Chen):**
> "The math works — ¥65K net contribution from the deal itself. Add 800 new user acquisitions at ¥42 AOV with a 25% repeat rate = ¥8,400 in incremental full-price GMV over 30 days. That's ¥73,400 total contribution for ¥50,000 investment. The problem is fulfillment. Shanghai can't handle 380 orders/day. But Jing'an and Pudong can each absorb 60 additional orders/day without stress. Total fulfillment cost: 200 orders × 14 days × ¥2 = ¥5,600. Net contribution drops to ¥59,400. Still worth it. I want to go ahead."

**Regional Store Manager (Chen, Shanghai):**
> "You want to send me 380 orders a day? That's not happening. Even with Jing'an and Pudong fulfilling their share, the quality risk is enormous. If we drop below 4.5 rating during the deal, Ele.me terminates the placement AND our ranking suffers post-deal. I'd cap deal fulfillment at 120 units/day from Shanghai (with Pudong handling the other 80), and negotiate with Ele.me for a 150-unit minimum instead of 200. At 150 units/day: total deal orders = 2,100, gross contribution = ¥49,308 minus fulfillment = ¥3,780, net = ¥45,528. Still positive. The volume guarantee is the real risk — what if it rains for 3 days and order volume drops?"

**Finance (Wang):**
> "I want to add a condition: the deal runs at 150 units/day minimum, but the brand-funded discount drops to 12% (¥9.12/unit) if Ele.me won't budge on the 200-unit floor. At 12%: total discount cost = ¥30,576, net contribution = ¥60,488 on deal alone. That's still good. Also: the 25% repeat rate assumption — that's based on our historical Ele.me conversion rate of 22%. We should set up tracking from day 1 to measure actual conversion, so we can make a data-driven decision on whether to renew the deal in Q4."

---

## Options Under Review

**Option A — "Full Commitment, Multi-Store Fulfillment"**
- Accept 200 units/day minimum, 14-day deal.
- Fulfill from Shanghai (180) + Pudong (60) + Jing'an (40) stores.
- Total logistics cost: ¥5,600. Net deal contribution: ¥59,400.
- Budget: ¥50,000 (fits). Quality risk: HIGH.

**Option B — "Negotiated Volume Floor"**
- Counter-propose 150 units/day minimum to Ele.me.
- If Ele.me rejects, walk away from placement — the margin is not worth the risk at 200 units.
- Use theEle.me competitive dynamic (Meituan also wants our brand) as negotiating leverage.
- Budget: ¥0 if Ele.me rejects.

**Option C — "Phased Deal with Quality Gate"**
- Run 14-day deal at 200 units/day minimum.
- Add a quality gate: if 3-day rolling rating drops below 4.4, deal pauses for 3 days for kitchen recovery.
- During pause, offer a limited 50-unit/day flash deal instead (lower volume, protects rating).
- Budget: ¥50,000 + ¥2,400 for flash deal subsidies.
