## Situation

A customer posted a video on Douyin (TikTok) at 9:47 AM today showing a foreign object in their Wok & Roll order from Store #7 (Hangzhou West Lake district). The video has:
- **0 hours old**: Already 47,000 views, 3,200 comments, 890 shares
- **Sentiment**: Overwhelmingly negative; top comments are calling it "food safety scandal," "disgusting," and several are tagging Hangzhou FDA and local news accounts
- **Account**: The poster (@foodie_hangzhou_777) has 85,000 followers; posts about food content regularly
- **Video quality**: The object is partially visible but not clearly identifiable — could be a plastic fragment, could be something else. The food container is clearly our branded packaging.
- **Our order data**: Store #7 had no reported food safety incidents on May 13. Customer order ID: #HZG-20260513-84732

We do not yet know if the foreign object is real, if it was introduced post-delivery, or if this is staged. The order was delivered via Meituan.

The Hangzhou FDA has received a complaint and has contacted Store #7. An inspector visit is expected within 24–48 hours.

## What We Know (Limited)

**In favor of genuine incident**:
- The customer has posted about food issues before (1 prior post about another brand, resolved)
- The container is clearly ours — not a spoof
- Order ID is valid and can be verified

**In favor of potentially staged/fraudulent**:
- Object visibility in video is poor — not definitively our food
- The poster's account has a pattern of "exposing" local restaurants (3 posts in 6 months, each gaining significant traction)
- Meituan's order confirmation photo (automatic camera at store) shows normal packaging

**What we don't know**:
- Was the food tampered with after delivery?
- Is the object from our kitchen, packaging, or customer-side?
- Has the customer contacted us directly yet? (They have not called our customer service line)

## Goals (Ranked)

1. **Contain the narrative within 24 hours** — prevent mainstream media pickup
2. **Avoid FDA enforcement action** — inspector visit going well is essential
3. **Protect brand reputation** — existing customers and potential customers should not permanently defect
4. **Support Store #7 team** — they are the frontline in this and are likely panicking
5. **Do the right thing by the customer** — if the incident is genuine, they deserve a real resolution

---

## Constraints

- **We cannot delete the video or legally threaten the poster** — Chinese consumer protection law protects food safety complaints and any retaliation will go viral
- **We cannot make public statements about the investigation** until we know more — but silence is also being interpreted as guilt
- **FDA response must be cooperative** — any appearance of obstruction will worsen the situation
- **We have 4 hours** to formulate an initial response before local media window opens
- **Social team is 3 people** — this is an all-hands situation

---

## Response Options Under Debate

**Option A — "Full Transparency, Immediate Recall"**:
- Immediately pull all potentially affected SKUs from Store #7's delivery menu
- Post on our official Douyin account: "We've seen the video. We take this seriously. We're investigating Store #7 immediately and have paused affected menu items pending inspection results. We'll share findings publicly."
- Offer direct contact to the customer for resolution
- Estimated response time: 90 minutes
- Risk: Pre-judges the incident before investigation; if staged, we've validated the narrative

**Option B — "Quiet Investigation, Measured Response"**:
- Do not post publicly yet. Focus on: (a) inspecting Store #7 internally, (b) contacting the customer directly, (c) reviewing Meituan order photos and kitchen footage
- Reply to the Douyin comment section with: "Hi, we've received your message and our team is reaching out to you directly to resolve this. Food safety is our top priority."
- Issue a brief public statement only after we have more information (even if 12–24 hours later)
- Estimated response time: 4–6 hours for substantive statement
- Risk: Silence for 4–6 hours gives competitor accounts and news time to amplify the story as "brand silent on allegations"

**Option C — "Counter-Narrative Play"**:
- Work with our campus KOC network (10 local Hangzhou food bloggers, 20K–100K followers) to post positive content about Store #7 — clean kitchens, happy customers, fresh ingredients
- Goal: flood the Douyin algorithm with positive content so the original video loses ranking
- Also reach out to the customer and media outlet for "private resolution" (if the incident is questionable)
- Estimated response time: 3 hours to get counter-content live
- Risk: If the incident is genuine, this approach looks like a cover-up and will make everything dramatically worse
