# ST-009: VIP Member Churn Risk — Top 500 LTV Tier

> **Priority**: HIGH — 60-day intervention window before permanent churn
> **Submitted by**: Membership & CRM Lead
> **Date**: 2026-05-11

---

## Situation

Our CRM system flagged an unusual pattern in the **top 500 LTV members** (avg ¥2,400/year, total annual value ¥1.2M). Over the past 60 days:
- Average order frequency dropped from 3.2 orders/week to 1.8 orders/week (-44%)
- Average order value dropped from ¥52 to ¥41 (-21%)
- 127 of the 500 have not ordered in 30+ days (approaching dormancy definition)

This is not a standard dormancy issue — these members have been with us for 18+ months and previously showed consistent engagement. They haven't churned (defined as 60+ days inactive) but are clearly cooling off.

Possible explanations (unconfirmed):
- New competitor in their delivery zone (3 new QSR options within 500m of our Shanghai cluster)
- Menu fatigue (same core items for 6 months, no new products in their order history)
- Quality perception gap (their recent ratings on our items have dipped from 4.7 to 4.4)

The CFO has allocated ¥30,000 for a VIP retention program targeting this group. The program needs to be profitable: target a 60-day LTV recovery to ≥ ¥380/member (¥190K total from 500 members).

---

## Goals (Ranked)

1. Recover ≥ 80% of flagged members (400/500) to ≥ 2.5 orders/week within 60 days
2. Maintain program CPA ≤ ¥75/member (¥30,000 / 400 retained = ¥75/member)
3. Identify and address the root cause of cooling — not just patch with discounts
4. Protect brand positioning — VIP members should not feel "bought back" with cheap offers
5. Build a reusable VIP early-warning playbook for future use

---

## Constraints

- **Budget**: ¥30,000 total for the program (incentives, content, events)
- **Discount rules**: Cannot offer more than 20% discount to any VIP member (brand positioning rule)
- **Data**: We have full purchase history, but no direct survey data on satisfaction reasons
- **Time**: 60-day window — if we wait 30 more days, many of these 127 near-dormant members will cross into dormancy
- **No mass discount**: The CFO specifically said no blanket 20% off — needs to feel premium, not desperate

---

## Team Positions

**Membership & CRM Lead (Zhang):**
> "This is a menu fatigue problem, not a price problem. These members have ordered the same 5 items for 18 months. The Hunan Mala bowl we tested — 87% like-it score — should have been launched 2 months ago. If we give them early access to new menu items before general release, that's a premium signal, not a discount bribe. I'd allocate ¥18,000 to a new-item exclusive access program (free delivery + priority kitchen for 90 days), ¥8,000 to personalized outreach from our brand account, and ¥4,000 to a small experiential event (a VIP tasting session for 50 of the highest-value members). This is relationship investment, not discount investment."

**Growth Manager (Chen):**
> "Menu fatigue is real but we don't know that's the root cause. We should A/B test: 200 members get the new-item exclusive access (my program), 200 members get a targeted ¥10 store credit (no minimum spend) — callable at any time in next 60 days. The ¥10 credit costs us nothing until redeemed, and it's a 'thank you for being a valued member' gesture, not a bribe. Track which cohort recovers faster and at lower cost. ¥30,000 budget: ¥0 allocated upfront, ¥20,000 held for the winning cohort."

**Finance (Wang):**
> "Both of you are spending money on hypotheses. The real question is: have these members actually churned? 127 have zero orders in 30 days — that's a 30-day zero. In our model, dormancy starts at 60 days. They're in a gray zone. I'd recommend a 'we miss you' outreach from our head chef (personalized WeChat message from our brand account), not an incentive. The message: 'We've noticed you haven't been ordering lately — is everything okay? Here's a new menu preview, and we hope to see you soon.' Zero cost. If they respond, we learn WHY. If they don't, we know they're truly churned. ¥30,000 stays in reserve for a targeted retention push only if the outreach identifies addressable reasons."

---

## Options Under Review

**Option A — "New Item Exclusive Access"**
- 90-day exclusive access to new menu items for all 500 VIP members.
- Free delivery + priority kitchen queue for these members.
- Personalized WeChat message from brand account introducing the program.
- Estimated cost: ¥18,000. Potential LTV recovery: ¥380/member × 400 = ¥152,000.
- Risk: Members who don't care about new items get no value.

**Option B — "A/B Test with Optional Credit"**
- Split 500 members: 250 get exclusive access program, 250 get ¥10 no-minimum store credit.
- Let members self-select.
- Allocate ¥30,000 to the winning cohort after 30 days.
- Estimated cost: ~¥2,500 in credits (if 25% redemption) + ¥3,000 in exclusive access ops.
- Risk: Data takes 30 days to be meaningful.

**Option C — "Relationship First, Incentives Second"**
- All 500: personalized WeChat outreach from chef/brand account (week 1).
- Based on response: segmented offer (menu preview + exclusive access for responders, store credit for non-responders at week 3).
- ¥30,000 held until week 3, then deployed surgically.
- Estimated cost: ¥0 week 1, ¥15,000 deployed in week 3-4.
