# ST-015: QSR Festival Campaign — Chinese Valentine's Day (Double Seventh)

> **Priority**: HIGH — Campaign window: July 27-30 (4 days), planning deadline: May 31
> **Submitted by**: Brand + Marketing + Growth
> **Date**: 2026-05-13

---

## Situation

**Double Seventh Festival (Qixi)** — Chinese Valentine's Day — falls on **August 3, 2026**. This is the second-largest gifting/date-night occasion after Valentine's Day in February, and QSR food delivery peaks significantly during this period.

Meituan data shows:
- QSR delivery volume on Qixi increases **38% above baseline** nationally
- Couples ordering together (2-person meals) increases **62% above baseline**
- Average order value increases **+22%** as couples order sides, drinks, and dessert together
- The competitive window is intense — every QSR brand runs Qixi promotions

Wok & Roll has never run a dedicated Qixi campaign. Our typical response has been reactive: we add a "Qixi Special" item to the menu and hope for the best. Results: +8% volume during Qixi week vs. market +38%.

The opportunity: a well-designed Qixi campaign could push us to +30-35% volume during the window — capturing the occasion-driven demand that currently goes to competitors.

**Timing context**: This is a premium date-night occasion. Our brand is a casual QSR. The positioning question is: does a QSR brand have a legitimate role in Valentine's Day? Yes, if we target the right segment — young couples (25-35) who see Qixi as a fun occasion, not a formal one.

---

## Goals (Ranked)

1. Achieve ≥ +30% volume growth during July 27-30 (Qixi window)
2. Drive ≥ +25% AOV increase (couples order more when marketed as a set)
3. Acquire ≥ 600 new delivery users during the campaign window
4. Maintain ≥ 58% gross margin during the campaign (promotional costs must be controlled)
5. Establish Qixi as an annual campaign anchor (repeatable playbook)

---

## Constraints

- **Budget**: ¥25,000 total campaign budget
- **Platform rules**: Meituan Qixi deals require minimum 15% discount on participating items
- **Fulfillment cap**: Shanghai store can handle 220 orders/day without stress (above our current 180 baseline)
- **Margin floor**: Cannot run any promotion below 58% blended gross margin
- **Timeline**: Campaign must be designed by May 31, confirmed with Meituan by June 7 to secure placement
- **Brand alignment**: Must feel festive and occasion-appropriate, not forced

---

## Team Positions

**Brand & Marketing (Lin):**
> "Qixi is a couples occasion. Our menu doesn't have a 'for two' story. I want to create a limited 'Qixi Date Night Box' — two Signature Wok Bowls + two drinks + a small dessert (chocolate) in a branded box at ¥88 (vs. ¥92 ordered separately). That's a 4% discount but it packages as a gift. Meituan will co-fund 50% of the discount (we pay 7.5%, platform pays 7.5%). We also run a WeChat Moments campaign targeting 25-35 year old couples in Shanghai with ¥12,000 spend. The campaign creates a brand moment — 'Qixi with Wok & Roll' — that extends beyond the transaction. Budget: ¥20,000 (Qixi box subsidy) + ¥12,000 (WeChat) = ¥32,000. Over budget by ¥7,000."

**Growth Manager (Chen):**
> "Qixi volume is real but the margin math is tight. The Qixi Date Box at ¥88 with 7.5% brand subsidy = ¥6.60 discount cost per box. At 220 boxes/day × 4 days = 880 boxes. Discount cost = ¥5,808. Plus WeChat spend = ¥12,000. Total = ¥17,808 — under budget actually. But I want to add an acquisition layer: any first-time buyer who uses a Qixi promo code gets ¥8 off their first order. Meituan co-funds 50% = ¥4 brand cost per new user × 600 new users = ¥2,400. Total: ¥20,208. Still under ¥25K. This captures both existing couples AND new users. Win-win."

**Finance (Wang):**
> "Both of you are ignoring AOV dynamics. On Qixi, couples who order without a deal average ¥68 AOV (2 bowls + sides + drinks). With a 'date box' deal at ¥88 for exactly 2 bowls + 2 drinks, we're actually DISCOUNTING their natural basket (¥92) by 4.3%. But if we create a 'Qixi Upgrade' bundle (2 bowls + 2 drinks + dessert + free delivery) at ¥98, we go ABOVE their natural basket. Customers feel like they're getting more (dessert, delivery) even though the bundle is priced at a small premium to individual items. That premium increases margin AND AOV. Estimated: 180 base orders × 22% AOV lift × 4 days + new user orders = ¥38,000 incremental GMV, ¥14,000 gross margin. Cost: ¥17,808. Net: -¥3,808. Actually negative ROI without the new user LTV."

---

## Options Under Review

**Option A — "Qixi Date Box + WeChat"**
- "Qixi Date Night Box" (2 bowls + 2 drinks + chocolate) at ¥88.
- Meituan 15% deal requirement met (we fund 7.5%).
- WeChat Moments targeting 25-35 couples (¥12,000 spend).
- No new user acquisition incentive.
- Budget: ¥20,208. Estimated volume: +35% (63 extra orders/day).
- Risk: Margin slightly negative on deal alone, offset by volume.

**Option B — "Qixi Date Box + New User Acquisition"**
- Same Qixi Date Box at ¥88.
- No WeChat spend — rely on Meituan placement and organic word-of-mouth.
- Add new user incentive: ¥8 off first order, brand-funded (¥4 after Meituan co-fund).
- Target 600 new users over 4 days.
- Budget: ¥5,808 (box subsidy) + ¥2,400 (new user) = ¥8,208. ¥16,792 held in reserve.

**Option C — "Qixi Upgrade Bundle"**
- "Qixi Upgrade Bundle" (2 bowls + 2 drinks + dessert + free delivery) at ¥98.
- No discount — premium positioning.
- Meituan placement under "Qixi Premium" category (higher visibility, less price-sensitive audience).
- No new user incentive.
- Estimated: +25% volume, +22% AOV, highest margin of the three options.
- Budget: ¥12,000 (WeChat creative + premium placement fee). Total: ¥12,000.
