# ST-006: New Menu Item Launch — Hunan Mala Signature Bowl

> **Priority**: HIGH — Q2 new product calendar, launch window June 1-7
> **Submitted by**: Product & Menu Team
> **Date**: 2026-05-10

---

## Situation

The R&D team has finalized the **Hunan Mala Signature Bowl** (¥42 AOV, 67% food cost, ¥13.86 gross margin per unit) — a new spicy/sour flavor profile that has tested well in Shanghai taste panels (87% like-it score, 62% "definitely would order").

We have two strategic choices for the launch push:

**Push-to-Known** (¥15,000 budget): Activate our WeChat member base (62,000 members nationally, 18,000 Shanghai). Send early-access notification to Shanghai members 3 days before public launch. Aim for 3,200 trial orders at ¥42 AOV = ¥134,400 GMV, ¥35,700 gross margin. Cost: ~¥4,200 in points incentives and free-delivery subsidies.

**Cold Acquisition** (¥15,000 budget): Use the new item as a loss-leader to attract new users via Meituan/Ele.me discounted trial (first-time buyer: ¥28 for the bowl). Target: 4,000 new user acquisitions. Lifetime value of a new delivery customer: ~¥340 over 6 months. Total LTV potential: ¥1,360,000. Cost: ¥14 discount × 4,000 = ¥56,000 — OVER BUDGET. Would need platform co-funding (Meituan will co-invest ~50% = ¥28,000, leaving ¥28,000 net cost to brand).

---

## Goals (Ranked)

1. Achieve ≥ ¥200,000 GMV from the new item in first 2 weeks
2. Acquire ≥ 1,500 net new delivery users (repeat-order-capable, not one-time deal-seekers)
3. Maintain ≥ 60% gross margin on the new item (pricing is fixed, so this means controlling incentives)
4. Establish the Hunan Mala flavor profile as a top-3 SKU within 60 days
5. Build reusable playbook for new item launches (so this decision framework can be reused)

---

## Constraints

- **Budget**: ¥15,000 total for launch campaign (points, delivery subsidy, content, influencer if used)
- **Platform rules**: Meituan does not allow free/discounted items that undercut the store's official menu price by more than 30%
- **Fulfillment**: Shanghai store can absorb +80 orders/day above current baseline without quality slip
- **Margin floor**: New item gross margin must stay ≥ 60% after all campaign costs
- **Co-branding**: No external brand partnerships on this launch (internal only)
- **Timeline**: Decision needed by May 15; launch window June 1-7

---

## Team Positions

**Product & Menu Lead (Zhao):**
> "Push-to-known is safer. Our 18,000 Shanghai members already trust us. We get predictable volume and decent margin. Cold acquisition is high-risk — ¥28 trial deals attract deal-seekers who never come back at full price. But the LTV math is undeniable. If Meituan co-funds 50%, the unit economics work. We just need to make sure the platform doesn't own the customer relationship after."

**Growth Manager (Chen):**
> "We have a strategic window here. The Hunan Mala flavor is genuinely differentiated — we've seen competitor copying attempts fail because they can't replicate the mala-to-sour balance. If we go pure push-to-known, we're leaving new user acquisition on the table and letting competitors observe what works for us. The hybrid approach: ¥8,000 to push-to-known (targeting Shanghai 18K members), ¥7,000 to a Meituan new-user trial (500 units at ¥30 each, platform co-funded). Total ¥15,000. Splits the difference and captures both cohorts."

**Finance (Wang):**
> "The hybrid approach needs scrutiny. ¥8,000 push-to-known: if 40% of Shanghai members respond (7,200 × 40% × 30% expected conversion = 864 orders), that's ¥36,288 GMV at 67% gross margin = ¥24,313 gross margin minus ¥8,000 cost = ¥16,313 net contribution. Fine. But the Meituan new-user program: 500 units at ¥30 sale price means ¥15,000 GMV at 67% margin = ¥10,050 gross, minus ¥7,000 cost = ¥3,050 net. Combined: ¥19,363 from ¥15,000 spend — a 1.29x multiplier. That's acceptable but not great. Is there a better channel for the same money?"

---

## Options Under Review

**Option A — "Push-to-Known First"**
- Launch with existing member base. Early access for Shanghai members (3-day window), then national rollout.
- No Meituan discount deal.
- Budget: ¥4,200 (points + delivery subsidy). ¥10,800 held in reserve.
- Estimated: 2,800 trial orders from existing members in week 1.

**Option B — "Cold Acquisition Sprint"**
- Use Meituan co-funded trial deal (¥28 first-timer price, platform covers ¥14, brand covers ¥14).
- Target: 2,000 new user acquisitions at ¥14 net cost each.
- Estimated: 2,000 new users, ~40% return rate at full price = 800 repeat orders in 30 days.
- Budget: ¥7,000 (brand portion of trial). ¥8,000 held.

**Option C — "Hybrid Launch Wave"**
- Week 1: Push-to-known (Shanghai members, early access)
- Week 2: Meituan trial (500 units, co-funded)
- Week 3: Influencer short-video push on Douyin (one food influencer, ~¥5,000 for 3 posts)
- Budget: ¥15,000 fully deployed across 3 channels.
