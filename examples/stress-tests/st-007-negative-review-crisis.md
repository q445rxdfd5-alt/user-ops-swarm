# ST-007: Food Safety Viral Crisis — Xiaohongshu Post

> **Priority**: CRITICAL — 72-hour response window before narrative solidifies
> **Submitted by**: Brand & Comms Lead
> **Date**: 2026-05-13

---

## Situation

At 14:32 yesterday, a Xiaohongshu user (@foodwatch_sh) posted a thread titled "Wok & Roll's Mala Noodles Made Me Sick — Don't Order This" with 6 photos of a stomach illness claim. As of 09:00 today:
- **53,000 likes**, 4,200 comments (mostly supportive of the poster)
- 1,200 reposts across Weibo and Douyin
- 3 local media accounts have picked up the story
- Our Meituan store rating dropped from 4.6 to 4.3 in 18 hours (47 one-star reviews)

The poster claims she ordered from our Shanghai Pudong store on May 10 and experienced food poisoning symptoms within 2 hours. She did not contact us directly. She posted publicly.

Our internal investigation:
- The Shanghai Pudong store had **no ingredient anomalies** on May 10. No other customers reported illness.
- The order number she shared is verifiable — order #WD20260510-4829, confirmed as our store.
- She did not submit a refund request through Meituan. She went straight to social media.
- The stomach illness claim is **medically unverifiable** from a social post. She has not provided any medical documentation.

The narrative is gaining traction because: (1) food safety is a hot-button issue in China post-2025 regulatory tightening, (2) the "mala" (numbing-spicy) flavor profile is associated with hygiene concerns in some consumer segments, and (3) our store has been aggressively promoted recently, making us a visible target.

---

## Goals (Ranked)

1. Stop narrative escalation within 72 hours (prevent 100K+ reach)
2. Protect Meituan store rating (current 4.3, floor before algorithm penalty = 4.0)
3. Avoid legal liability — do not admit fault without evidence
4. Maintain team morale — store staff feel unfairly targeted
5. Build a reusable crisis playbook for future social media incidents

---

## Constraints

- **Legal**: Cannot publish statements acknowledging fault without legal sign-off. A premature apology can be used as evidence in civil proceedings.
- **Platform**: Meituan's crisis protocol requires us to notify them within 4 hours of a food safety allegation or they may auto-suspend our store pending review.
- **Discount ceiling**: Cannot offer mass refunds/discounts as crisis response without triggering platform review of our account.
- **Time**: We have until end of day tomorrow (36 hours) before the story reaches media tipping point (estimated 80K likes = media coverage threshold).
- **Evidence gap**: We cannot prove definitively that our food was safe, only that we found no anomalies. The burden of proof in the court of public opinion is different from legal court.
