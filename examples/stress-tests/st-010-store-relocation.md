# ST-010: Store Relocation Announcement — Shanghai Lujiazui Branch

> **Priority**: MEDIUM — Move date: July 15, announcement needed: May 25
> **Submitted by**: Regional Ops + Brand
> **Date**: 2026-05-13

---

## Situation

Our Shanghai Lujiazui branch — located at Shanghai World Financial Center B1 since 2022 — has had its lease terminated by the property management. We must relocate 800 meters to a smaller unit at Shanghai International Financial Center (IFC) Mall, also B1 level. The new location is 800m from the current one, has 60% of the current floor space, and opens August 1.

Key data on the current store:
- **Volume**: 180 orders/day (60% delivery, 40% walk-in/office pickup)
- **Customer mix**: 70% regular office workers (Lujiazui financial district), 30% foot-traffic tourists/visitors
- **Member base at this store**: 4,200 registered members whose default pickup address is "Wok & Roll SWFC"
- **Delivery radius**: Covers 22 office buildings — our densest delivery cluster
- **LTV**: ¥680/year per member (high — office district)
- **Rent**: Currently ¥28,000/month. New space: ¥34,000/month (+21%)

The move creates a specific risk: the 4,200 members who registered with "SWFC" as their home address may assume we are permanently closed when our SWFC store goes dark (July 15 - July 31). Our new store at IFC opens August 1. That's a 16-day gap with no physical presence in Lujiazui.

---

## Goals (Ranked)

1. Retain ≥ 90% of the 4,200 Lujiazui member base through the transition
2. Maintain Meituan ranking in the Lujiazui delivery corridor (we have top-5 ranking, at risk during 16-day gap)
3. Complete member address migration without mass unsubscribe (previous relocation of our Beijing Sanlitun store lost 38% of local members)
4. Control incremental cost: new store setup + member migration campaign ≤ ¥45,000
5. Use the relocation as a brand narrative opportunity (upgrade, expansion signal)

---

## Constraints

- **Timing**: Announcement must go out May 25 (10 days from now). Legal requires 30-day notice to members under Chinese consumer protection rules for service discontinuation.
- **16-day gap**: No physical store July 15 - July 31. Meituan does not allow "temporarily closed" status for more than 7 days without delisting consideration.
- **Member data**: We can send WeChat notifications to all 4,200 members, but email and SMS require updated consent records.
- **New store capacity**: 60% of current space = 108 orders/day max (vs. current 180). We need to set expectations.
- **Rent increase**: ¥6,000/month = ¥72,000/year additional cost. New store must generate ≥ ¥6,000/month incremental revenue to break even on rent.

---

## Team Positions

**Regional Ops Manager (Chen):**
> "The 16-day gap is the killer. Meituan's algorithm will drop our ranking if we're dark for more than 7 days. I need to either (a) keep taking delivery orders through a dark store (impossible), (b) fulfill from our nearest store (Pudong, 4km away — delivery time goes from 28 min to 52 min, customers will complain), or (c) pause Meituan entirely for 16 days and restart August 1 with a 'grand reopening' campaign. Option (c) loses ¥28,000 in delivery GMV but protects ranking if Meituan doesn't delist us. Option (b) risks rating damage from delivery delays. We need a decision this week."

**Brand & Marketing (Lin):**
> "This is a brand story opportunity. We're not closing — we're moving and upgrading. The narrative should be: 'Wok & Roll Lujiazui is evolving — our new IFC location is bigger, better, and opens August 1.' I want a full member migration campaign: WeChat notification May 25 with a QR code to update delivery address, a ¥5 discount on first IFC order as a welcome-back incentive, and a social media announcement. Budget: ¥8,000 in discounts + ¥12,000 in campaign costs. The key metric: how many of the 4,200 members update their address before August 1."

**Finance (Wang):**
> "The rent increase needs to be justified. New space at 60% size but +21% rent means we need higher AOV or higher margin per order to break even. Current: 180 orders × ¥42 AOV × 61% margin = ¥4,618/month margin. New space: 108 orders max × ¥X AOV × 61% = must exceed ¥4,618 + ¥6,000 = ¥10,618/month. Solving: AOV must be ≥ ¥162 to break even. That's not realistic for a QSR. We either need to (a) negotiate the new rent down, (b) find a way to keep volume at 180 by adding delivery-only model at the IFC location, or (c) accept this is a brand investment, not a profit center."

---

## Options Under Review

**Option A — "Full Member Migration Campaign"**
- May 25: WeChat notification to all 4,200 members, QR code + ¥5 welcome discount + clear new address.
- July 1: Reminder notification with countdown to new store opening.
- August 1: Grand reopening push with a limited-edition new bowl.
- 16-day gap: pause Meituan entirely, accept GMV loss, restart fresh August 1.
- Budget: ¥18,000. Estimated member retention: 85-92%.

**Option B — "Dark Store Delivery Pivot"**
- Keep Meituan live July 15-31 by fulfilling from Pudong store (4km away).
- Accept 52-minute delivery times, offer 20% discount on all orders during gap to offset delay complaints.
- Meituan ranking preserved. Rating risk: high. Budget: ¥6,000 in gap-period discounts.
- New store August 1: standard opening, no grand campaign.
- Budget: ¥6,000. Risk: rating damage from delivery delays.

**Option C — "Hybrid: Pause + Accelerate"**
- Pause Meituan July 15-25 (10 days). Resume July 25 for soft opening.
- August 1: grand reopening campaign.
- Run aggressive member migration May 25-July 15 (50-day runway).
- Budget: ¥12,000 in member migration campaign + ¥8,000 reopening.
