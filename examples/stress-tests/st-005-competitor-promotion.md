# ST-005: Competitor Promotion Counter-Strike — Wok & Roll

> **Priority**: HIGH — 48-hour response window
> **Submitted by**: Regional Delivery Lead
> **Date**: 2026-05-12

---

## Situation

At 09:00 this morning, **Ma Kitchen** — a direct competitor in the Shanghai Pudong delivery corridor — launched a "50% Off Flash Deal" on Meituan. The deal runs for 72 hours. Their store is 1.2km from our Wok & Roll Shanghai branch.

Based on past experience, we can expect:
- **~18-22% volume drop** on our Shanghai store during the deal window
- **~25% of affected customers may not return** even after the deal ends
- At our current order volume (220/day × ¥38 AOV = ¥8,360/day), a 20% drop over 3 days = ~¥5,000 in lost GMV

We have 48 hours to decide whether to respond, how aggressively, and through which channel.

---

## Goals (Ranked)

1. Limit volume loss to ≤ 10% during the 72-hour deal window
2. Protect brand positioning — we are not a discount brand
3. Maintain blended margin ≥ 58% (our floor for delivery channel profitability)
4. Retain at least 90% of affected customers post-deal
5. Do not set a precedent that we match competitor discounts reactively

---

## Constraints

- **Budget ceiling**: ¥8,000 for any counter-campaign
- **Margin floor**: Cannot run any promotion that drops blended margin below 58%
- **Discount ceiling**: Combined brand + platform discount capped at 30% per order
- **Time constraint**: Decision needed by 18:00 tomorrow (33 hours from now)
- **Fulfillment capacity**: Our Shanghai store can handle 260 orders/day max — currently at 220, so we have ~40 units of buffer capacity
- **Do not**: Run a matching 50% off (margin-destructive and brand-damaging)

---

## Team Positions

**Regional Delivery Lead (Jiang):**
> "We have to match something. Our regulars see the Ma Kitchen deal on Meituan's homepage. If we do nothing, we're telling our own customers that Ma Kitchen is better value. Even a targeted 15% off to our last-30-day buyers through WeChat membership notification would blunt the bleed. We have the margin room — we shouldn't be passive."

**Brand & Marketing Manager (Lin):**
> "We built Wok & Roll on quality positioning. Responding to every competitor's discount move destroys that. We should let our product do the talking. If we drop to 50% off, what do we say next time? 40%? This is a race to the bottom. We should do a targeted message to our most loyal members instead — something that reinforces quality, not one-upmanship on price."

**Finance Controller (Wang):**
> "¥8,000 is our budget ceiling. A 15% off coupon for 3 days to our entire member base (18,000 members) would cost roughly ¥12,000 in foregone margin — over budget. We need to be surgical. Target only the Shanghai store's delivery radius and only the 3 deal days. If we can do a geo-targeted 10% off limited to 500 redemptions, that's ¥1,900 in margin cost — well within budget."

**Regional Store Manager (Chen, Shanghai):**
> "I can handle 260 orders. Right now I'm at 220. If we drive volume I can absorb it without quality slippage — kitchen is not the constraint. My concern is staff overtime. If volume spikes above 250, I need approval for 2 extra shifts (¥600/night). That's not in the current budget."

---

## Options Under Review

**Option A — "Surgical Geo-Target"**
- 10% off, geo-targeted to Shanghai delivery radius, limited to 500 redemptions
- Meituan homepage placement not sought
- Estimated cost: ¥1,900 in margin foregone
- Pros: Preserves brand positioning, hits back at competitor quietly, within budget
- Cons: Only captures incremental volume from existing customers, not new acquisition

**Option B — "Membership Loyalty Wave"**
- 15% off via WeChat member notification to Shanghai members only (6,200 members)
- No Meituan platform deal — stays within brand positioning
- Estimated cost: ~¥3,800 in margin foregone (avg order ¥38 × 6,200 × 50% expected redemption × 15%)
- Pros: Rewards loyal members, reinforces WeChat ecosystem, stronger retention signal
- Cons: Higher cost, targets existing base not new customers at risk

**Option C — "Quality Counter-Narrative"**
- No discount. Instead: run a WeChat content campaign ("Why Wok & Roll doesn't do flash sales") with a limited-edition new bowl at full price, plus free delivery
- Estimated cost: ¥2,200 (content production + delivery subsidy on 500 new-bowl orders)
- Pros: Strongest brand signal, no margin sacrifice, captures the "quality over discount" segment
- Cons: Highest risk — doesn't directly address the volume loss, content campaign ROI is hard to measure in 3 days
