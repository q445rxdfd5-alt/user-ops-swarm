# ST-012: Pricing Audit — Margin Erosion on Core Menu Items

> **Priority**: HIGH — Ingredient price increases effective June 1, decision needed by May 20
> **Submitted by**: Finance Controller + Product Team
> **Date**: 2026-05-09

---

## Situation

Our supplier has issued revised pricing effective June 1 due to upstream agricultural cost inflation:

| Item | Current Cost | New Cost | Current Price | Current GM% | GM% at Current Price |
|------|-------------|----------|-------------|-------------|---------------------|
| Signature Wok Bowl (base) | ¥11.20 | ¥13.80 | ¥38 | 70.5% | 70.5% |
| Mala Noodle Set | ¥14.80 | ¥17.40 | ¥46 | 67.8% | 67.8% |
| Deluxe Lunch Set | ¥18.60 | ¥19.20 | ¥58 | 67.9% | 67.9% |
| Premium Beef Bowl | ¥22.40 | ¥26.80 | ¥58 | 61.4% | 61.4% |
| Signature Wok + Drink | ¥14.20 | ¥16.80 | ¥46 | 69.1% | 69.1% |
| Sides (3 items avg) | ¥4.80 | ¥6.20 | ¥18 | 73.3% | 73.3% |

All items except Deluxe Lunch Set and Premium Beef Bowl will fall below our **60% gross margin floor** if we maintain current prices.

We have 3 strategic options:

**Option 1 — Raise prices**: Increase all affected items by 8-12%. Risk: volume drop. Our price elasticity in the delivery segment is approximately -0.8 (1% price increase = 0.8% volume drop).

**Option 2 — Absorb costs, shrink portion**: Reduce portion sizes slightly to maintain price and margin. Risk: customer perception / negative reviews.

**Option 3 — Rebrand as premium**: Introduce a "Quality Upgrade" narrative with minimal price increase (3-5%) on select items, emphasizing sourcing quality.

We need to decide by May 20 to update Meituan and Ele.me menu pricing before June 1.

---

## Goals (Ranked)

1. Maintain blended gross margin ≥ 60% across all items post-June 1
2. Limit order volume impact to ≤ 5% from price/portion changes
3. Communicate price changes in a way that doesn't trigger negative review campaigns
4. Develop a dynamic pricing framework for future cost fluctuations (so we don't have this crisis every 6 months)
5. Preserve brand positioning — we are not a budget brand

---

## Constraints

- **Platform pricing rules**: Meituan requires 48-hour notice for menu price changes. Ele.me requires 72-hour notice.
- **Competitor context**: 3 competitors in our delivery corridor have raised prices 5-8% in the past 90 days. Consumer tolerance for QSR price increases is historically moderate (post-pandemic, consumers are more price-sensitive).
- **Volume data**: Our base delivery orders = 180/day. A 5% volume drop = 9 orders/day × ¥42 AOV × 365 days = ¥137,970 annual GMV at risk.
- **Finance floor**: Cannot absorb cost increase without price change — the gap is ¥2.60-4.40/unit across 5 items.
- **Timing**: Decision by May 20, implementation June 1. 10-day window.

---

## Team Positions

**Finance Controller (Wang):**
> "We have to raise prices. The math is simple: at current prices with new costs, the Signature Wok Bowl goes to 63.2% gross margin — below our 60% floor. On 180 orders/day with 40% Wok Bowl mix = 72 units/day × 365 days × ¥2.60 margin gap = ¥68,472/year we're leaving on the table. Raise prices 8% on the Wok Bowl (to ¥41) and 6% on Mala Noodle Set (to ¥49). Volume impact at -0.8 elasticity: 8% price × -0.8 = -6.4% volume = 11 fewer orders/day. Annual GMV impact: -11 × ¥42 × 365 = -¥168,630. Net: we save ¥68,472 in margin but lose ¥168,630 in volume = -¥100,158. We need a better option."

**Product & Menu Lead (Zhao):**
> "What if we reduce portion sizes 12% on the items with the worst cost increases (Wok Bowl and Premium Beef Bowl)? Maintain current price. The cost savings: Wok Bowl: 12% smaller = ¥1.34 saved/unit × 72 units/day = ¥96/day × 365 = ¥35,040/year. Premium Beef Bowl: 12% smaller = ¥3.22 saved/unit × 38 units/day = ¥122/day × 365 = ¥44,530/year. Total savings: ¥79,570/year. This works on margin without touching price. Risk: customers notice and feel cheated. We can frame it as 'right-sizing for freshness' or 'quality-controlled portions.'"

**Brand & Marketing (Lin):**
> "Both of you are thinking inside the box. We're a quality QSR brand, not a commodity bowl shop. The rebrand option: raise prices 5% across the board, but spend ¥15,000 on a 'Farm-to-Bowl Quality Commitment' content campaign — explain the ingredient cost increases honestly, highlight the specific sourcing improvements, and position the price as a reflection of quality, not greed. We did a similar thing in Q3 2024 with a 6% price increase — volume dropped only 2.1% because the narrative was right. That was a 4% elasticity. If we can repeat that, 5% price × -0.5 elasticity = -2.5% volume = -¥38,325 annual GMV impact. Meanwhile we gain: 60%+ gross margin maintained, brand equity reinforced."

---

## Options Under Review

**Option A — "Raise Prices, Accept Volume Impact"**
- Raise Wok Bowl to ¥41 (+8%), Mala Noodle to ¥49 (+6.5%), Premium Beef to ¥62 (+7%).
- Estimated volume impact: -6.4% = 11 fewer orders/day.
- Annual net impact: -¥100,158 (volume loss > margin gain).
- Communicate via in-app notification + menu update note.

**Option B — "Portion Adjustment"**
- Reduce Wok Bowl portion 12%, Premium Beef portion 12%.
- Maintain all current prices.
- Cost savings: ¥79,570/year. Volume impact: minimal (0-1%).
- Risk: negative review risk if customers perceive shrink.
- Communicate as "freshness-controlled portions" or similar.

**Option C — "Quality Rebrand + Moderate Price Increase"**
- Raise all items 5%.
- Run ¥15,000 content campaign: "Why our prices are changing" (transparency narrative).
- Estimated volume impact: -2.5% = 4-5 fewer orders/day.
- Annual net impact: +¥22,145 (margin gain offsets volume loss).
