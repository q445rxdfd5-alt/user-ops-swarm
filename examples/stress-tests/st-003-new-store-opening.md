# ST-003: New Store Opening — Nanjing Cold-Start Acquisition

> **Priority**: HIGH — Store opens June 15, user acquisition plan needed now
> **Submitted by**: New Markets Expansion Lead
> **Date**: 2026-05-12

---

## Situation

Wok & Roll is opening Store #48 in Nanjing (Xianlin University Town) on **June 15, 2026**. This is our first Nanjing location and our first attempt at a "campus-adjacent" market segment. The store is 200 sqm, 32 seats, full kitchen, delivery-capable.

Unlike our existing stores, this is a **cold-start situation**: no existing member base, no brand recognition in Nanjing, no delivery data for the specific catchment area.

The catchment area contains:
- Nanjing University of Science & Technology (NUST): ~30,000 students, 18–24 age range
- Nanjing Normal University: ~22,000 students
- 4 residential compounds: ~8,000 households, mixed income
- Office park (newly built, ~40 companies, ~2,000 office workers)
- 6 competitor QSR/wok shops within 800m

Nanjing consumer profile differs from our Shanghai cluster:
- Average ticket size is ¥28–¥32 (vs ¥38–¥45 in Shanghai)
- Strong preference for value bundles and group ordering
- Heavier Ele.me usage than Meituan (estimated 40/40/20 split vs Shanghai's 65/30/5)
- Student market is highly price-sensitive but also highly social — word-of-mouth and group ordering are dominant

---

## Goals (Ranked)

1. Generate 800 first-time customers in the first 30 days (post-opening)
2. Build a local member base of 1,500 in 60 days (post-opening)
3. Achieve break-even on store P&L within 90 days
4. Establish delivery ranking in Nanjing's "Asian Wok" category within top 15 by Day 30
5. Generate organic UGC (social mentions, reviews) without paid influencer budget

---

## Constraints

- **Pre-opening budget**: ¥25,000 (covers soft opening, local promotion, launch event)
- **Ongoing monthly marketing budget**: ¥8,000/month
- **No dedicated store staff for marketing**: GM + 6 crew only
- **Store opening promo ceiling**: Cannot exceed 35% combined discount on opening offers
- **Platform rules**: Meituan and Ele.me both require store to be fully operational for 7 days before running promotional campaigns
- **University access**: Can distribute flyers on campus with advance approval (2-week lead time required)
- **Student discount policy**: We can offer student discounts but they must be verifiable (student ID or campus card)

## Options Under Debate

**Opening Strategy A — "Campus Invasion"**:
- Partner with 10 campus KOC influencers (students, 5K–20K followers each, ¥300–¥800/post)
- Free taste tests at campus entrance over 3 days before opening
- "Bring your student card, get 20% off for 30 days" permanent student discount
- Estimated new customer acquisition: 1,200 in 30 days
- Estimated cost: ¥18,000 pre-opening + ¥5,000 KOC fees
- Risk: ¥25K pre-opening budget overrun; student discount permanently lowers ticket size

**Opening Strategy B — "Platform Launch Special"**:
- Open quietly on June 15, spend first 2 weeks building delivery fulfillment rate
- Start with 20% off limited menu on Ele.me (our strongest Nanjing platform)
- Hold grand opening event on July 1 (after platform compliance period)
- Use saved pre-opening budget for targeted platform ads
- Estimated new customer acquisition: 650 in 30 days (lower volume, higher quality)
- Estimated cost: ¥8,000 pre-opening + ¥12,000 platform ads
- Risk: Slower member base building; competitor captures mindshare in critical first 2 weeks

**Opening Strategy C — "Community First"**:
- Focus on the 8,000 residential households near the store (not students)
- Door-to-door flyer distribution in all 4 compounds, with QR code to mini-program
- "Opening family bundle" (4-person meal, ¥88 = 15% off regular price)
- Partner with residential property management for WeChat community group promotion
- Estimated new customer acquisition: 550 in 30 days, but higher LTV (families vs students)
- Estimated cost: ¥6,000 pre-opening + ¥4,000 community promotion
- Risk: Slower ramp, but builds sustainable neighborhood customer base

## Critical Question

Given the cold-start nature, student vs. residential audience trade-off, tight budgets, and platform constraints — what is the optimal go-to-market sequence for June 15 opening, balancing speed of acquisition with customer quality and sustainable unit economics?

## Anti-Goals

- Do not launch a massive discounting war with Lao Mao's — we cannot win a price war against an established local favorite
- Do not open without delivery capability — in this market, delivery is expected from day one
- Do not ignore the office park segment — 2,000 office workers at ¥35–¥40 tickets are higher-value than students
- Do not over-discount opening offers — we need to avoid training Nanjing customers to expect 30%+ discounts
